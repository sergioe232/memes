Before we start...

When building a website, it is important to understand the basic level functionality of how a website works and what it needs to function appropriately. 

A website runs on something called a server. A server is the physical machine computer that hosts your website's content and distributes it to people all over the world. One of the issues in owning a server or renting a server a server from a third-party is that servers are expensive :(

A website also needs code in order for the server to properly display and distribute content to the users. Reinventing the wheel by developing code that will do this is unfortunately very time consuming, and again expensive :(

Luckily, we do not have to do any of this. There are countless third-party website hosting companies like Wordpress, Wix, Weebly, and many other platforms that provide excellent professional website building tools that cut out the need to code everything.

On top of that, many of these platforms will host your website on their servers entirely FOR FREE.





Setting up the Website

For this example, we are going to create a website using Wordpress.


Step 1.

Go to wordpress.com. 

Click on the "Create your site" button.

You should have a form that looks similar to the picture below.

Fill out the information in the form with what most applies to you.




Step 2.

Decide on a name for your site and input that name into the main text box.

If the name is available it will allow you to choose it; if not, then you can either choose a different name, or an alternative name from the choices wordpress gives you.

If you want to choose a .com name, you will have to pay money. (Note. we will go over .com's and domains later on in this guide)

However, for now we will choose the "xxxxx.wordpress.com" (xxxxx being the name you chose to name your website) name that word press gives us for free.

Click the "Select" button of the free site option.

Input your personal email address, and desired username and password for the new Wordpress account we are creating. Press the "Continue" button.

Go to your personal and check for a verification email that was sent by Wordpress.

Click the blue "Click here to Confirm Now" button, it will activate your new wordpress account and redirect you to your website.

So we have managed to create our own personal Wordpress website entirely for free. YAY :D

our website is called websitename.wordpress.com instead of websitename.com

Why is that? 

A bit of background on Domains

Every big website on the internet, whether its Google.com, Facebook.com, Wikipedia.org, etc, has a domain. 

Lets take the example "https://www.google.com"

This phrase that you type in at the box at the top of your browser window is called a URL address

This URL address tells your browser "hey, take me to the Google website".


The part that comes after the "http://www." part of the URL is the domain, which in this example is "google.com".


A domain is super important, whether your a small blogger or a mega corporation. The domain is what gives your website its identity. If you want your website to stand out and you want people to find your website easily, then that means you are going to need to set up and register a domain name. Registering domain names typically cost money. However, here is a very simple and quick way to register your own domain name for absolutely free.



Registering your free domain name

Step 1.

Go to freenom.com. You should be greeted by a large white text box and a large blue button that says "Check Availability". This is where you are going to check if your unique name is available. 

Type your desired domain name in the text box and click check availability to see if it is available. If your desired name is not available, try and think of a different name.








Step 2.

Once you have found an available name, you should be presented with several options. For my example, I chose bulletcade.tk for my name. However, if you wish to choose any of the other suffixes when choosing your personal name, that is perfectly fine as well. If you wish to choose the popular .com suffix for your domain name, that is perfectly fine but be warned; the .com suffix is not free and will cost anywhere from $8 to $12 a year depending on the site where you register your domain.






Step 3.

After choosing our specific name, we are given a cart that tells us which domain names we wish to purchase. Since freenom offers free .tk domains I will not have to pay anything to purchase it (However if we chose the .com name in Step 2 rather than the .tk, we would have ended up having to pay that $8.38 fee). 


Pro Tip: One of the perks that freenom and many domain registration sites offer is free Domain ID Protection AKA WhoIsPrivacy. When you buy and register a domain, you are required to put your name and address in the registration, which means anyone who visits your site can see your full name and address. Fortunately, this is where Domain ID Protection comes in; Domain ID Protection is when the domain registration company uses their personal information in place of your own in order to keep your privacy safe. Be warned, even though freenom.com and other domain registration companies offer Domain ID Protection for free, there are also many domain registration companies, such as GoDaddy.com, that will charge you extra money in order to activate the Domain ID Protection.


Click the "Continue" button to move onto the next step.





Step 4.

As we can see, we won't be charged a dime in order to register our domain name.

Simply input your personal email address into the box that says "Enter Your Email Address" and click the blue button that says "Verify My Email Address".


Login to your personal email account, you should have an email from freenom that contains a verification link.

Click on the verification link.





Step 5.

The link should have taken us to a page requesting us to input some personal information to complete the order. Make sure to fill out all the boxes in the form if they apply.

Don't forget to check the "I have read and agree to the Terms and Conditions" before clicking Complete Order, otherwise you will have to fill in the boxes again.

Finally click the blue "Complete Order" button.

Combining your Domain Name and Website

So now that we have both our Wordpress website and our own personal domain name here, our next objective is to combine the two.

We want to set up our website so that when people enter our custom domain, they are taken to our Wordpress website, but our users will not see the ugly yoursitename.wordpress.com URL in their browser address bar, instead they will see the custom domain name we created earlier in this guide.




               

Setting up the Domain for the combination







  • Login to the freenom.com account we made earlier in the guide
  • Click on "Services" to open the drop down menu
  • Click on "My Domains"
  • Click the "Manage Domain" button
  • Click on "Management Tools" to open the drop down menu
  • Click "Nameservers"
  • Make sure "Use default nameservers (Freenom Nameservers)" is checked and click the "Change Nameservers" button
  • Click on "Manage Freenom DNS"






Edit the pre-existing record so that it shares the same Name, Type, and TTL (NOTE: SET TTL TO 600 instead of 1, 1 will cause an error) as the picture above. However, for target, make sure target is the personal Wordpress site that you created in this guide. yoursite.wordpress.com (MAKE SURE THERE IS NO HTTPS:// or www.)


Click "Save Changes".

It will take roughly 10 minutes for the record to take into effect because we set the TTL to 600 seconds. However, after the 10 minutes, we are done messing with the domain. :D



Now to setup the Website for the combination


Step 1. 

Login to your Wordpress account so that you are on your site's control panel, as we were previously in this guide.

In the left hand side of your screen you will see a list, scroll down that list until you see an option called "Domains"

Click on the "Add" button next to "Domains".



Step 2. 

Go to the option that says "Already own a domain?" and click the "Use a domain I own" link at the very end.


Step 3.

Click the "Transfer to WordPress.com" button


Step 4.

Enter the name of your owned domain with a www.

For example ww.yoursite.com

Click the "Transfer" button



Finally, we are finished!

It will take several minutes for the changes to update all across the internet, but we finally own our own website outfitted with our own custom domain.

Now when people type in your custom domain, they will be taken to your website. As a bonus, even if your users type in your old xxxxx.wordpress.com address, they will still be redirected to your website under your custom domain in the browser address bar.
